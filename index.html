Heterobasidion Root Disease in Wisconsin

History: Discovery and Spread

Heterobasidion root disease has been an economic problem in the southern United States since the 1940s. It wasn't until 1993 that it was discovered in a pine plantation in Wisconsin and has since been found in 23 counties in the state.

Stand Level

At the stand level expanding forest gaps can be seen scattered amoungst the rectalinear rows of the red pine plantation. Over time these gaps grow creating yearly rings of dead trees propagating from the orginal infection site. Salvaging trees surronding the gap only works to accelerate the spread of the disease as it will gladly feed of the necrotic tissue of the stumps and roots.




WDNR - Risk and Recomendations

The WDNR condsiders areas within 25 miles of known infection sites as hight risk areas. In these areas special consideration should be taken when managing vulnerable species. Areas located between 25 and 50 miles from known infection sites are condsidered medium risk. Some considerations sould be made in medium risk areas, though management is not as difficult. Areas located 50 miles or more away from infection sites require little to no special considerations.
